Project Idea

Overview
The concept for this project centres around using a Raspberry Pi connected to a heat sensor that will turn on the air conditioner when a pre-determined temperature is reached. When activation occurs an email and/or text message can also be sent to a nominated contact. The targeted user would be the elderly or disabled who still live in their own homes in Australia, as having their air conditioning turn on automatically would assist in preventing them over-heating and the message sent to the nominee contact, will remind get in contact with the person to check on their well-being.

Motivation
The care of the independently living elderly and disabled in Australia should be of high priority in summer due to the intense heat caused by climate change, as temperatures are expected to increase in future years as detailed by a report published by Climate.gov

Since Australian summers do get quite hot, people will often leave it until the last minute to turn on their air conditioning. Leaving it until a room it feels uncomfortably warm increases the probability of an elderly or disabled person putting their health or life in jeopardy.

Issues
Should the Raspberry Pi have a software or hardware failure or the device loses power and the air conditioner does not turn on this can be countered by incorporating a 'Deadman' alert and 'all clear' alert.
In addition to the 'Deadman'/'all clear' alert it could be set up that the Pi would send a message at a pre-determined regular time to inform the nominated contact that the system is functioning correctly. If a message is missed, it will be a sign to check the system.

There would also be the issue of cost of running an air conditioner. Many people may not wish to have air conditioning enabled by an automatic system due to concerns over cost of electricity. So to counter this the use of solar technology would be a suggested requirement.

The idea of home automation might be perceived by some people as enabling laziness, but used in the right way it can be of huge benefit. For example, for someone who may have mobility issues, the automatic nature of the device to turn on the air conditioner would be a benefit to their well-being and health, as well as a source of comfort for family members who may worry about their elderly parents or disabled family member. It could also be useful to integrate with other technology such as a virtual assistant such as Google Assistant, Apple Siri, or Amazon Alexia.

If the system works as expected for a hot summer, the system could then be adapted to also work in winter to enable the heat to be turned on. Due to the small cost of the Raspberry Pi and the free nature of the Linux operating system, additional units could be used in multiple locations in a home to detect where hot or cold spots are and only enable the heating or air conditioning in those rooms that require it. This is also where the use of wireless would be of benefit as the devices with the sensors could be relocated if required.

Tools and Technologies
A Raspberry Pi is a small single board computer that can be expanded with the use of peripherals or modules to enable extended functionality. The main circuit board can be housed in various models of container boxes. The Pi runs on a Linux based operating system. There are non-Linux based alternatives for the Pi, even a Windows based variant called Windows 10 IoT Core, with IoT standing for 'Internet of Things'. Use of the Linux based system would ensure that support is easily available as the Raspberry Pi has a strong following in the Linux based community and therefore functionality could be expanded or development assisted.

Raspberry Pi devices can operate via a wired or wireless connection. While wireless connection enables better setup and placement, this can also be a point of failure. A wired connection tends to be more reliable but the need to have a long cable running to devices would not be practical in a lot of situations. The air conditioning unit installed in the home should also be compatible with wireless option. Supplement to this idea would be the use of assistant enabled power points to also allow the use of this technology with pedestal or desk fans. It all ties into the idea of a smart home technology however the use of a Raspberry Pi with its heat sensor would make it all automatic.

The heat sensor itself is not integrated with the Raspberry Pi in its basic form so a separate module unit will need to resourced and integrated. The variant of this model would need to be investigated to see which offers the most cost-effective solution to obtain and allows for ease of programming with the Raspberry Pi operating system.

Outcome
The outcome of this technology would be a reduction in the cases of heat related deaths of the independently living elderly or disabled people. This technology would also provide peace of mind for family members.
